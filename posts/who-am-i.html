who am i?
does one really know?
mayhap nobody does.
natheless destitute of the verity —
confined i calmly grow.

an apprentice?
a turgid penman?
maybe a rowdy teenager?
assuredly one of them.
if not, subsequently—
i am scarcely a skimpy stranger.

i've worn faces i didn't stitch.
spoken words i didn't mean.
yet somewhere in the misfit pile —
a self, half-buried, half-serene.

i experiment stuffs.
plentiful of them.
some clinch triumph, some fail.
still i proceed, still i arise.
not sanctioning the defeat prevail.

and perhaps that is the answer.
not a name, not a craft —
but the one who keeps returning,
to himself, fore and aft.
