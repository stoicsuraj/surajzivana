i hate you.
i hate the day our eyes met.

i hate the way you smirked.
when i fell to the ground,
like you had already guessed
i was the type who trips over feelings.

i hate how you offered your hand,
so casually heroic.
i hate how i let you pull me up,
as if i couldn't stand on my own.

i hate how you praised my nerdy mind,
how you made my overthinking
sound like brilliance.
you had no right to make me feel rare.

i hate that you loved me,
for who i was then.
confident, ridiculous, invincible.

i hate that when i was at my best,
you weren't beside me.
such a nice thing, isn't it?

i hate the way you started the conversations.
i would've waited.
i always wait.
but you ruined my patience.
i hate you for that too.

i hate how you tolerated, my footballing genius,
my dramatic celebrations,
my need to win everything,
including you

i hate that you asked me out.
i hate that i said yes,
like i wasn't already halfway gone.

i hate that evening.
i hate how easily we laughed.
i hate how we confessed,
as if we discovered something real.

i hate the people we became,
after that evening.
so certain,
so sure that this would last.

i hate how we spent all that time,
how we adapted to each other,
how we built new habits around each other,
how your name became a reflex in my mind.

i hate that you left.
and ultimately, i hate you.
i definitely do.

but sometimes, in solitude i think.
is it hate?
or just my pride,
bruised and pacing in circles,
angry that i fell for you
without calculating the risk?

i hate you nevertheless.
or maybe i just hate losing.
i hate it.
