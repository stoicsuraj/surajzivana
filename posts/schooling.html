They say every journey has a beginning, but mine didn't start with success, trophies, or praise. It began with a quiet fall, a bruised ego, and a lesson disguised as a conversation. Looking back, I call that moment Phase Zero, the point where growth began, not from winning, but from self-realization.

Being an overachiever in everything I did in life, I was pretty overconfident for someone of my age. Thinking my parent knew nothing, and wanted me to pursue life as they deem to and make my life decisions. I had a slight hatred towards teachers too, always thought that they targeted me but I think that was due to my behaviour and lack of self-awareness. This went on till I was promoted to Grade 9. It was all good till I was an A-student, but then something happened.

My grades went low from my perspective, I despised studying and was heavily consuming ideas from internet. The shift from studying to gaming wasn't kind to me. Although, I brought good grades without that much of studying, I always knew that I am not doing good. The proudness that I had behold was slowly crippling me.

One day, returning to school after my morning classes, I bumped into the Principal of the school, a proclaimed educator, being an A-student, he obviously knew me. Also, he had taught my siblings, that is another reason too. He shared a beautiful example with me, Imagine a tree as a person, and the fruits it bears as the knowledge it holds. The more fruits the tree has, the more it bends toward the ground. Likewise, the more knowledge a person gains, the more humble and grounded they should become. I knew what he was trying to elaborate indirectly and I grasped the insight of it too. With a small example of the tree, my Principal gave me a great example that day, which I still follow in my life.

From that day, I was a slightly different person. Although, I did have disagreement with the teachers occasionally, because they deemed me wrong sometimes. Even then, I always respected them — except for a few individuals who couldn't stand me. I did good in my studies and passed my Grade 10 with a good number, still I knew I had the potential to do better. I still think I should've given it for re-checking, but as they say — You can't pour new dreams, into old times.

And that was it, the end of my schooling. I walked away from that chapter of my life not just with grades or certificates, but with a little bit of wisdom, a bunch of friends, and a heart full of memories.
