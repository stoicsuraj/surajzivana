Jan 18, 2026

the square stands like an old witness,
tired of explaining itself.
bricks stacked with centuries of belief,
cracked not by time,
but by people passing through
without ever staying.

i stood there too,
just an ordinary figure,
watching vendors arrange their days in small piles,
coins, silence, patience.
jeevan yahi ho,
their hands seem to say,
not bitter, just aware.

tourists drift by with their foreign eyes,
romanticizing the view of the square.
they look for meaning in temples,
while locals look for hope in tomorrow.
between them hangs a quiet misunderstanding,
polite but permanent.

faces pass me.
some softened by hope,
some sharpened by loss,
some undecided about their path.
every smile rehearsed,
every stillness full of drama.
man bhitra kura khelirako cha,
even when the mouth stays still.

we call this a crowd,
but it is a gathering of solitudes.
each person thinking differently,
living and loving differently,
hurting in shapes no one else recognizes.
what saves one, destroys another.

i remembered,
even those who say they know me
only know the version that survived.

they have never sat with my thoughts at night,
never felt the weight of a sentence
before it is spoken.
and when i try to explain it,
my elaboration fractures,
spoken not as i intended,
but as they are capable of hearing.

because understanding is not shared truth,
it is interpretation.
samjhauna khojda pani,
my thoughts lose their original language,
become something safer,
something smaller.

perhaps this is the tragedy of being human.
not the solitude,
but the impossibility of full translation.
even love misreads,
even closeness approximates.

so i kept walking.
entire lives unfolding side by side
in the square,
touching briefly, never merging.
complex, unfinished,
hoping to be understood,
slowly learning that no one ever fully will.

and still, the square remains there
watching me try,
watching you try,
and watching us try.
